Academic Studies Supporting SDG 11

Below are some of the significant findings from recent studies
Urban Resilience and Sustainability

Recent studies emphasize the importance of integrating resilience strategies, such as green infrastructure and disaster preparedness, into urban planning. These strategies are crucial for cities to withstand the increasing risks associated with climate change (Rozhenkova et al., 2019).

Affordable Housing and Inclusivity

Research highlights that inclusive housing policies, such as mixed-income developments, are effective in reducing urban inequality and fostering social cohesion in cities (Smets & van Lindert, 2016).

Read More
Sustainable Transportation

Studies have found that cities adopting sustainable transportation solutions, including electric buses and bike-sharing systems, experience reduced carbon emissions and improved public health outcomes (Schraven et al., 2021).

Read More
Urban Green infrastructure

Research into urban green infrastructure (UGI) shows its vital role in supporting sustainable development by enhancing ecosystem services, improving air quality, and promoting social well-being. Integrating UGI into urban planning is essential to achieving the goals of SDG 11 (Hanna & Comín, 2021).

Philippine National Urban Development and Housing Framework (NUDHF)

This framework shows the country's strategic approach to urban development, emphasizing sustainable housing, infrastructure, and public services to address rapid urbanization challenges.

National Climate Change Action Plan (NCCAP)

This report emphasizes the integration of climate adaptation strategies into urban planning to build more resilient communities, particularly in disaster-prone areas (CCC, 2021).

Strategies to Improve Urban Planning

With the growth of technology, developing smart cities ensures improved quality of life and greater sustainability and prosperity empowering citizens. The Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) established the ASEAN Smart Cities Network (ASCN), a collaborative platform where cities from the 10 ASEAN Member States work together to achieve a smart and sustainable urban development.

Case Studies of Successful Initiatives

Cauayan City

• Cauayan City has laid the groundwork for becoming a smart city through various initiatives across Infrastructure, Data, and Service phases. Since 2014, the city has installed WiFi in 65 barangays and collaborated with telecom companies for fiber optic and cellular network expansion. Sustainable initiatives include e-tricycles and e-scooters, developed with support from universities and government agencies. The city had also lauched a centralized WiFi network for barangays and implemented telemedicine during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Tuguegarao City

• Tuguegarao City is making strides in smart city development, focusing on Infrastructure, Data, and Service phases. The city provides free internet in public areas and offices through partnerships with Globe Business and DOST. The MyCure System offers online medical consultations, and a Command Center monitors real-time activities with CCTV in collaboration with local authorities. Additionally, the Tuguegarao Government Portal enables access to various online services, including travel passess, online payments, and vaccination registrations.

San Fernando City

• San Fernando City is advancing in smart city development, focusing on Infrastructure and Service phases. The city has established free fiber-powered WiFi in key areas like the City Hall and Old Public Market, supported by Smart Communications. Fiber optic cabling and solar panels are also part of the city’s infrastructure improvements. In the Service phase, the Command and Control Center, inaugurated in 2020, enhances city management with advanced monitoring systems. Additionally, the city offers online business registration, with an option for delivery service, streamlining processes for residents and businesses.

Davao City

• Davao City is enhancing its smart city development with major initiatives across Infrastructure, Data, and Service phases. Infrastructure projects include the High Priority Bus System, Underground Distribution System, and advancements like RT-PCR machines and biodiesel technology. Data initiatives focus on traffic enforcement with cameras. Service improvements feature Central 911 for emergencies, Safe Davao QR for travel passes and contact tracing, and online business permit applications. These efforts aim to boost infrastructure, data management, and overall quality of life.
